About

Kofoworola School of Informatics is a non-profit apprenticeship program. The Apprenticeship program is a software engineering training that provides the talent need in the technology space in Nigeria. The goal is to equip apprentice to fill that demand for more female software engineers through skills-based, on-the-job learning and training.

Kofoworola School of Informatics is an important vehicle for helping individuals reskill and make a career switch. The Apprenticeship combines formal learning, a self-study program that involves in-person and virtual training. The in-person training involves a bi-weekly physical meetup in Lagos.

The Apprenticeship provides professional development for each apprentice, allowing each apprentice in the program to learn and earn at the same time. Upon successful completion of the apprenticeship, each apprentice will have the opportunity to convert to full-time employment.

Frequently Asked Questions



How frequent will the meetups be?

The on-site training will be lab sessions which is for every two weeks on saturdays in a month.

Who can Apply?

The Apprenticeship is only open to university graduates and equivalent and targeted towards women.

Do I have to pay?

The Apprenticeship is sponsored such that each apprentice does not have to pay to learn but the other way around.

Where will the program take place?

The program will take place in Lagos and it is open to Lagos residents for ease of access especially for Lagos mainland.

How is the payment mode for the apprentices?

Each successful apprentice into the program will be paid a stipend from the second month of apprenticeship.

What's next after the program?

Each graduate apprentice will be given the opportunity for full employment with KSOI after graduation.
